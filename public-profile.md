## About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at The University of Hong Kong with an interdisciplinary background in architecture, the built environment, human behavior, and AI-assisted decision systems.

My current research focuses on developing a closed-loop AI framework that identifies behavior-related performance problems, generates context-aware interventions, evaluates their effects, and refines intervention strategies through feedback.

I am interested not only in whether an AI system can answer a question, but also in how it can identify priority problems from evidence, generate actionable interventions, and improve them through structured human feedback.

## Core Research Project

### Closed-Loop AI Nudge System for Human-Building Performance Alignment

My Ph.D. research develops an AI-enhanced framework for identifying, predicting, generating, and evaluating behavioral interventions related to building performance.

The proposed workflow includes:

1. behavior-pattern discovery and target identification;
2. spatio-temporal behavior prediction;
3. performance-impact evaluation;
4. LLM- and RAG-assisted intervention generation;
5. human and multi-agent evaluation;
6. immersive experimentation and feedback-based refinement.

## Current Prototype

### TIF Core

TIF Core is a lightweight prototype for domain RAG systems:

**Evidence → Target Discovery → Intervention Generation → Human-in-the-Loop Feedback**

The first version will:

- accept structured context and retrieved evidence;
- identify one to three actionable targets;
- generate structured intervention candidates;
- validate required evidence, triggers, steps, and risks;
- collect field-level human feedback;
- save reusable structured records.

The building-operation scenario is the first example, rather than the identity or final boundary of the system.

## Distinctive Strengths

My main strength lies in connecting AI system development with human-centered problem formulation and evaluation.

Rather than focusing only on whether an LLM can generate an answer, my research considers:

- which problem should be prioritized;
- whether the retrieved evidence supports the decision;
- whether the proposed intervention fits the context;
- whether it is actionable, ethical, and reviewable;
- how human feedback can support continuous refinement.

This perspective allows me to contribute to domain RAG and Agent systems through the combined viewpoints of knowledge organization, behavioral intervention, human-AI interaction, and evaluation.
